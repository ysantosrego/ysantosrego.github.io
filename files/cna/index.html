About

I am a postdoctoral researcher (wissenschaftlicher Mitarbeiter) in the Geometry Group of Prof. Petra Schwer at the Otto-von-Guericke Universität Magdeburg.

I defended my PhD at the Universität Bielefeld under the supervision of Prof. Kai-Uwe Bux. In Brasília I was also a member of the groups PETMat-UnB and Theory of Computation under the guidance of Profs. Mauro Rabelo and Maurício Ayala-Rincón, respectively.

Research interests

Roughly speaking, my research concerns properties of spaces and of their symmetries. The fun part is that the term "space" can be interpreted quite broadly, from a hairy ball to a comic book-like multiverse known as Bruhat-Tits building, from fundamental geometric shapes to model spaces describing the motion of a robot.

For example, for the space we could ask whether there are serious obstructions there (such as holes), whether it is curved somehow, or if we can describe what will happen to an object in it moving around under certain rules.
As for the symmetries themselves, we might want to know how many are there, whether we can describe them in a simple fashion, how they transform objects in the given space, or even about the relationship between given sets of symmetries of the same space.

In mathematical terms, my work so far has been mostly in combinatorial group theory and related topological topics, with particular interest in presentations, finiteness conditions, and algorithmic questions. As such, I am very fond of geometric, topological, homological, and combinatorial aspects of groups and spaces on which they act.

Groups that I like include (but are not limited to) matrix groups (algebraic, Lie, (S-)arithmetic,...), R. Thompson's groups and their relatives, locally compact groups, and Artin-Tits groups. I am also interested in formal methods in mathematics.
